# AI-Driven Forecasting for Sustainable Operations

### Demand Prediction & Energy Consumption Forecasting

## Overview

This project was developed as part of an academic initiative focused on **integrating AI into operations to enhance sustainability in European manufacturing firms**.

The core objective was to design forecasting models that enable companies to:

* Anticipate **future demand**
* Predict **energy consumption**
* Optimize **resource allocation**
* Reduce operational waste and inefficiencies

By leveraging time series forecasting, this project demonstrates how AI can support **data-driven, sustainable decision-making in industrial environments**.

---

## Problem Statement

Manufacturing companies face two critical challenges:

1. **Demand uncertainty** → leads to overproduction or stockouts
2. **Energy inefficiency** → increases costs and environmental impact

This project addresses the question:

> *How can forecasting models improve operational planning while supporting sustainability goals?*

---

## Solution Approach

Two forecasting use cases were developed:

### 1. Energy Consumption Forecasting

* Predict future energy usage based on historical patterns
* Identify **seasonality and peak consumption periods**
* Support energy optimization strategies

### 2. Demand Forecasting

* Predict product demand over time
* Enable **better production planning and inventory management**
* Reduce overproduction and waste

---

## Modeling Approach

### Model Used

* Prophet

### Why Prophet?

* Captures **trend, seasonality, and holiday effects**
* Robust to missing data and outliers
* Fast to implement and interpret
* Well-suited for **business-oriented forecasting use cases**

---

## Methodology

1. Data preprocessing and cleaning
2. Datetime structuring and indexing
3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
4. Time series decomposition (trend & seasonality)
5. Model training using Prophet
6. Forecast generation
7. Model evaluation using performance metrics

---

## Model Evaluation

Model performance was assessed using standard forecasting metrics:

* RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error)
* MAE (Mean Absolute Error)
* MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error)

These metrics ensured that predictions were not only visually accurate but also **quantitatively reliable**.

---

## Key Insights

* Energy consumption exhibits strong **seasonal patterns** and recurring peaks
* Demand signals show **cyclical behavior linked to time-based factors**
* Forecasting enables **proactive planning instead of reactive operations**
